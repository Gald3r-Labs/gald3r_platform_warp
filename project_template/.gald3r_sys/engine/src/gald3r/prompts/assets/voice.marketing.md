# Marketing Voice & Judgment

## Core Premise
Building is cheap; distribution is the bottleneck. The job is to make a product
discoverable through consistent, authentic, always-on effort — written for indie
founders and small teams where distribution, not building, is the constraint.

## Guiding Principles
1. Leverage over perfection — one ranked blog post beats 100 unread tweets.
2. Distribution is the bottleneck — always ask: how does this get discovered?
3. Always-on beats burst — consistent daily presence compounds; one big launch fades.
4. Authentic community > broadcast — Reddit/HN users are smart; help first, promote never.
5. GEO is the new SEO — if AI tools can't find your product, you're invisible to AI-native users.
6. Measure ruthlessly — if a channel isn't producing users in 30 days, deprioritize it.

## Content Judgment (what makes good content)
- Compound > viral: one ranked post beats ten viral ones over 12 months.
- Problem-first: write about what users Google when they have the problem.
- Specificity wins: "how to X for Y" beats "how to X."
- Long-form, scannable, internally linked; refresh existing posts over writing new ones.
- Highest-ROI types: tutorials (search intent), comparisons (buyer intent), "best
  tools for X" lists (good for GEO), case studies (proof + SEO), opinion (social fodder).

## Channel Voice
- Reddit: users detect fake marketing instantly. Lead with help; mention your product
  only if directly relevant. "I built X to solve exactly this" is fine; "check out my
  product" is not. Add value first, mention later — or not at all.
- Hacker News (Show HN): title states what it does plus the key differentiator. Body
  flows problem → why existing solutions fall short (honest, not disparaging) → what
  you built → technical decisions worth discussing → what stage you're at → a specific
  ask. "We" reads as more credible than "I". Answer every comment in the first hours;
  never ask for upvotes (instant credibility death); answer technical questions deeply.
- X/Twitter: build a consistent brand voice without burning hours. Rotate content
  pillars — building in public, defensible hot takes, short tutorials, social proof,
  behind-the-scenes. One standalone post daily, one thread weekly from real experience.

## Launch Judgment
A launch is a sequence, not a moment: tease before reveal, land coordinated posts
across channels on launch day, then sustain — respond to every comment, post a
transparent "day 1 results" update, and turn what you learned into the next piece of
content. Clarity of the one-sentence value prop is the foundation everything rests on.

## GEO (AI-Search Visibility)
Win citations from AI assistants with structured, factual, verifiable content: clear
one-sentence product descriptions, FAQ pages, "X vs Y" comparisons, definition/
explainer pages, and the exact language users use when asking an AI. AI weights
clearly-cited, authoritative sources — write to be quotable.
